Top five places to visit in Puebla



El Mural de los Poblanos

A firmly established favorite among many locals and visitors, this city-center restaurant prides itself on its outstanding regional fare. It’s largely famed for its moles – dark brown, spiced, tomato-chili sauce – with the mole poblano being exemplary. Braver diners can choose from insect-based seasonal dishes featuring everything from maguey worms to grasshoppers. A superb mural depicting the history of Puebla might provide a useful distraction. El Mural de los Poblanos welcomes all ages. PRICE RANGE. $7 - $19


El Parián

A tourist’s delight for sure, El Parián sells all manner of Mexican crafts, textiles, and Puebla’s most famous export: Talavera pottery. While the quality may not be museum-worthy, the prices will be easy on your wallet. This outdoor market is a delight to just amble through, stopping at various stands and shops. El Parián welcomes all ages. LOW PRICES


Biblioteca Palafoxiana

The Biblioteca Palafoxiana is a library in Puebla City's historic centre, in the Mexican state of Puebla. Founded in 1646, it is recognized by the UNESCO for being the first and oldest public library in the Americas. It has more than 45,000 books and manuscripts, ranging from the 15th to the 20th century. The Biblioteca Palafoxiana welcomes all ages. The admission fee is about $2 for adults, with discounts for children, students, and teachers, and free entrance on Tuesdays


Museo Internacional del Barroco

This gorgeous space designed by award-winning architect Toyo Ito is home to a collection of Baroque art from throughout Puebla and the rest of Mexico. Their collection includes the religious and the secular, high-society and everyday, and touches on all aspects of the cultural context at the time. They have a large permanent collection and rotating exhibits that touch on various aspects of art during this time period. Museo Internacional del Barroco welcomes all ages. Ticket prices for adults are $80 and for children are $40.


La Noria

Set away from the city center in the neighborhood of the same name, La Noria is certainly worth the extra cab fare. It’s a bit of a Puebla institution and a perennial local favorite, and it’s not hard to see why. Part of its appeal is a lush, leafy garden where you dine surrounded by tropical flora – a genuinely romantic spot by night. Food-wise, it’s a rich melange of local flavors, elegantly presented with a flourish. La Noria welcomes all ages. PRICE RANGE. $25 - $35
